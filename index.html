About

The name Arihant has the literary meaning "one who does not have any enemy", which is so true about my son Arihant who is now a 20 years old adult on the autism spectrum.

Arihant foundation is my humble dedication to my son Arihant and many others like him who have not only taught me unconditional love, compassion and patience, but steered me to translate these qualities as my mission.

I thank all those live who I have touched in my last sixteen years journey in the field of special education to make me take up this immense challenge of providing quality facilities to the special ones.

Looking forward to your support in my journey to make their lives as happy and meaningful as possible.

Services

Early Intervention Programme

Where young children are supported by one to one therapies, children in the age group 3-5 years are placed in this programme. Parents are guided to continue the therapy in the home settings so as to maximise the benefits.

Under this programme, home visits are organised to help parents structure the environment and give parents the guidance to optimise learning in the home environment. Counselling of parents is provided to foster acceptance and maximise learning for the young children undertaken by trained professionals.

Navmitra - School for Children (Ages 5-12)

Children are placed in small groups of 4-5 who are taught by a special educator assisted by a helper. IEPS (Individualised Education Plans) are written for the children on a quaterly basis and reviewed every quarter during regular parent and teacher interactions.

Group Programmes

Targets the play and social behaviours of a small group of children where various pretend play activities are organised. Emphasis is laid on turn taking games and activities which are motivating and exciting for the children. Skills such as waiting and group behaviour are targeted in this programme.

After School Therapy

OT, Speech and Physiotherapy, Sensory Integration

Remedial Classes

Remedial classes are provided by competent teachers who are equipped with teaching students of mainstream schools who have a diagnoses of LD or Slow Learners.

Respite Care

Day boarding and respite care facilities are provided to families.

Pre-vocational and ADL training for young adults

Prevocational and Independent Work Skills Training is provided for children in the age group 8 onwards to make them equipped to follow a vocation independently as they reach adulthood.

ADL Training (Activities of Daily Living) is an integral part of our programme and lots of emphasis is laid on these skills. Activites in this programme include Independent Skills Targeted Eating, washing, cleaning, grooming, washing clothes (using machine), cooking for self, laying table, house keeping skills.

Vocational Training

Vocational training is provided to young adults (age group 18+) in areas of their interest. Training in packaging, block printing, soap making, chocolate making, baking, lamination, use of coffee and tea vending maching, pottering plants etc.

Mission

To be a source for the children, adults and their families to lead lives filled with joy, pride and acceptance, and to strive for their inclusion in all walks of life.
